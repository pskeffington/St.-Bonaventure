# Mass-Day Photography Checklist

## Purpose

This checklist organizes a single Mass-day photography shoot for first-phase inventory and identification. The objective is to produce a complete visual record of the St. Bonaventure collection as displayed, section by section, without creating white-background object photographs.

The shoot should support:

- section mapping;
- display-order control;
- visible-label transcription;
- theca-front review;
- uncertain-name triage;
- later catalog population.

The shoot should not attempt:

- authentication claims from image evidence alone;
- removal of sealed contents;
- publication-grade white-background photography;
- rearrangement into thematic or devotional categories;
- object cleaning, repair, or conservation work.

## Roles

| Role | Action |
|---|---|
| Lead photographer | Captures all required images in order. |
| Recorder | Tracks section, row, position, filename sequence, and uncertainty notes. |
| Handler or parish contact | Opens cases or permits access only where appropriate. |
| Reviewer | Checks blur, glare, missing labels, and missed sections before teardown. |

One person may hold more than one role, but the photographer should not be the only person responsible for the written inventory notes.

## Required equipment

- primary camera or phone with sufficient storage;
- backup camera or phone;
- charger and power bank;
- microfiber cloth for camera lens only;
- small notebook or printed shot log;
- pencil or pen;
- small paper session card;
- portable light only if permitted and non-disruptive;
- no flash during Mass or devotional activity;
- no white-background setup for Phase 1.

## File and session control

Photograph a session card before the first collection image.

Session card should include:

Photographer:
Recorder:

## Section 1 - Arrival and permission check

Actions needed:

- Confirm where equipment may be placed.
- Confirm whether any case may be opened.
- Confirm whether flash, portable light, or tripod use is permitted.
- Confirm whether photography must stop before, during, or after Mass.
- Identify one parish/contact person authorized to answer access questions.
- Photograph the session card.
- Start a written shot log.
- Do not begin close photography until access boundaries are clear.

Output:

- session card image;
- access note in shot log;
- list of any restricted sections.

## Section 2 - Pre-Mass context pass

Actions needed:

- Photograph the room or chapel context from the entrance.
- Photograph the full display wall or reliquary location.
- Capture left, center, and right wide views.
- Capture any nearby title card, parish note, label, or display explanation.
- Avoid disturbing worshippers, candles, devotional objects, or liturgical preparations.

Output:

- full-location context images;
- full-reliquary context images;
- visible environmental notes relevant to provenance and arrangement.

## Section 3 - Mass-time rule

Actions needed:

- Stop all close photography during Mass unless explicit permission has been given.
- Do not use flash.
- Do not move around the sanctuary or display area during the liturgy.
- Use Mass time for written review only if seated and non-disruptive.
- Mark any unfinished sections for post-Mass completion.

Output:

- no required collection images;
- written list of remaining sections.

## Section 4 - Post-Mass setup reset

Actions needed:

- Reconfirm access with the parish/contact person.
- Re-photograph the full display before touching or opening anything.
- Confirm that all cases are in their original displayed order.
- Assign one section at a time before moving into close shots.
- Keep bags, papers, and equipment out of frame where possible.

Output:

- post-Mass full-display image;
- section order note;
- updated access note if permission changed.

## Section 5 - Whole-display control images

Actions needed:

- Photograph the entire display straight-on.
- Photograph the entire display from the left angle.
- Photograph the entire display from the right angle.
- Photograph the top, middle, and bottom bands of the full arrangement.
- Confirm that every case or horizontal section appears in at least one sharp image.

Output:

- global section map images;
- reference images for later physical-order reconstruction.

## Section 6 - Case-by-case inventory pass

For each case or major horizontal section, complete the same sequence before moving to the next section.

Actions needed per case:

- Photograph the full case straight-on.
- Photograph the left half of the case.
- Photograph the right half of the case.
- Photograph the top row.
- Photograph the middle row if present.
- Photograph the bottom row.
- Photograph any case label, hinge, lock, frame, or divider that helps map the section.
- Recorder writes the section name, rows present, and obvious display order.

Output:

- complete case image set;
- row count;
- section boundaries;
- physical-order notes.

## Section 7 - Row sequence pass

Actions needed per row:

- Photograph the entire row from left to right.
- If one image cannot capture the full row sharply, use overlapping images.
- Keep neighboring labels visible where possible.
- Take a second angled row image if glare is present.
- Recorder assigns row number and approximate position count.

Output:

- row-level sequence images;
- stable row and position framework;
- note of glare, occlusion, or unreadable labels.

## Section 8 - Individual position pass

Actions needed per visible display position:

- Photograph object/theca and label together.
- Photograph object/theca centered.
- Photograph label centered.
- Photograph neighboring left and right context if position order is ambiguous.
- Do not combine two labels into one record unless the display clearly indicates one object.
- Do not split one object into multiple records unless physically distinct thecae or labels support it.

Output:

- one provisional object candidate per display position;
- label/object pairing evidence;
- display-order continuity.

## Section 9 - Visible-label transcription rescue pass

Actions needed:

- Review all labels on camera before leaving.
- Re-shoot every blurred label.
- Re-shoot every label with glare.
- Re-shoot every partial or cropped label.
- Capture alternate angle for curved, glossy, or shadowed labels.
- Mark uncertain readings in the shot log instead of correcting them silently.

Output:

- clear label images where possible;
- uncertain-label list;
- partial-label list;
- duplicate-name review list.

## Section 10 - Theca-front pass

Actions needed:

- Photograph the front of each visible theca where safely accessible.
- Capture theca plus display label together.
- Capture close view of any visible inscription, internal paper, thread, seal, wax, or decorative border.
- Do not open or disturb sealed objects.
- Do not infer authenticity from the photograph alone.

Output:

- theca-front image set;
- visible-inscription candidates;
- seal/thread visibility notes;
- follow-up list for inaccessible or obscured thecae.

## Section 11 - Document and provenance note pass

Actions needed:

- Photograph any visible authentication paper, envelope, accession note, parish record, or catalog card if already accessible.
- Photograph document with surrounding context first.
- Photograph full document.
- Photograph readable close-up sections.
- Do not separate documents from objects unless authorized.
- Record which object or case the document appears to accompany.

Output:

- document context images;
- readable document images where possible;
- document-object linkage notes;
- restricted-document follow-up list.

## Section 12 - Final completeness check before leaving

Actions needed:

- Confirm every section has full-case, row, position, and label images.
- Confirm every row has left-to-right sequence coverage.
- Confirm every visible label has at least one close image.
- Confirm uncertain labels are listed.
- Confirm any skipped section has a reason.
- Confirm original arrangement is restored.
- Photograph the full display after completion.
- Photograph final session card or end marker.

Output:

- completed shot log;
- final display image;
- missing-view list;
- follow-up photography list.

## Minimum shot log fields

```text
session_id
section
case_id
row
position
view_type
photo_sequence
visible_label_raw
confidence
left_neighbor
right_neighbor
glare
blur
occlusion
handled_status
notes
```

Recommended confidence values:

```text
clear
probable
partial
illegible
not_visible
```

Recommended handled-status values:

```text
not_handled
case_opened_only
object_lifted
object_rotated
unsafe_not_attempted
unknown
```

## End-of-day outputs

After the shoot, produce:

- original photo archive preserved unchanged;
- renamed working-copy image set;
- photo session manifest;
- section map notes;
- provisional display-position inventory;
- visible-label transcription table;
- uncertain-label review table;
- missing-view follow-up list;
- next-shoot priorities.
