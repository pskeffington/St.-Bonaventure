# Reliquary Section Map

## Basis of sectioning

The reliquary is divided here by physical case and visible label sequence, not by devotional importance, saint rank, relic class, or authentication status. The uploaded photographs show long, horizontally mounted wooden cases with red fabric lining, brass plates, and individual thecae. The display appears broadly alphabetical by saint name, with some compound or group labels.

## Immediate visual conclusions

The collection is not a single small devotional reliquary. It is a wall archive of multiple long display cases. The red backing, consistent brass labels, and repeated theca mounts indicate a curated display system. The organizing principle appears to be a label-sequence catalog, probably alphabetical. The case labels should therefore be indexed in physical order before any theological rearrangement is attempted.

## Confidence grading

- High: brass labels that are fully legible in close-up images.
- Medium: labels visible in angled case photographs but partially cut off or distorted.
- Low: paper theca inscriptions, abbreviated Latin labels, and labels partially outside the frame.
- Not verified: relic class, authenticity, saint identity where label and theca disagree, provenance, donor history, and certificate metadata.
