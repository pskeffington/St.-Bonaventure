# St. Bonaventure Reliquary Archive Note

This archive documents a Catholic reliquary associated with St. Bonaventure and containing a large collection of saint relics displayed in illuminated wooden cases. The photographed installation shows a structured devotional archive rather than an isolated reliquary object: multiple wall-mounted cases, red fabric backing, brass labels, and individually mounted thecae arranged in an apparent alphabetical sequence.

The first cataloging pass identifies physical sections and transcribes visible labels. This is not yet an authentication register. The visible display labels provide the first level of evidence. The paper inscriptions within the thecae provide a second level. Certificates, seals, parish records, and provenance documents will provide the third and controlling level for any future claim about authenticity, relic class, or chain of custody.

The current working index confirms a visible St. Bonaventure label within the B-series case, near S. Blandina and S. Bernard of Clairvaux. The broader display includes saints, martyrs, popes, founders, Doctors of the Church, missionary martyrs, and compound groupings such as Cecilia, Valerian, and Tiburtius; Jacinta and Francisco Marto; Canadian Martyrs; and Paul of the Cross with Philip Neri.

This repository will proceed in layers: physical mapping, visual transcription, theca transcription, historical notes, certificate verification, and publication synthesis.
